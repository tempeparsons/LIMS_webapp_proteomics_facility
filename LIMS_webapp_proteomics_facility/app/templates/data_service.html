Data Analysis at CIMR


The CIMR proteomics facility also provides a data analysis service, which can be requested on the 'Experiment Request' form independently of sample analysis, or as an optional add-in with a sample submission. The facility runs the most popular proprietary proteomics software and can accommodate most open source, freeware or user-provided software compatiable with Linux or Windows operating systems.

We can perform any level of analysis from simply providing lists of identified proteins, to processing data through ready-made pipelines in software such as Proteome Discoverer or Perseus, to creating a bespoke analysis method in R or Python, with extended analysis using AlphaFold2, UCSF Chimera etc. and finishing with publication-ready figures. Analysis methods tend to be unique to each experiment, so it's a good idea to contact the facility and disucss your requirements.

The facility runs the following proteomics software:
  • Mascot
  • Proteomics Discoverer
  • Peaks
  • Scaffold
  • MaxQuant
  • DIANN
  • Skyline
  • Perseus
